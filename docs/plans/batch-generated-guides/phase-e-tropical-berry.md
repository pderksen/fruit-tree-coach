# Phase E — Tropical + berry per-task guides

Date, Mango, Guava, Mulberry, Pawpaw. The final phase of the
per-task rollout. This group has the thinnest US-extension coverage
of any phase — Mulberry and Pawpaw are already flagged in
`all-phases.md`'s Revisit section as having thin coverage. The plan
is to lean hard on the sources that DO exist (UF/IFAS for tropicals,
UNR + UA for date palm, Ohio State + Penn State + UGA for mulberry
and pawpaw) and ship fewer guides per tree rather than invent advice.

## Trees in scope

- **Date** (`Phoenix dactylifera`) — dioecious palm, female trees
  need hand pollination to fruit, strand-thinning + bagging are the
  home-grower management jobs, prune dead fronds in January (not
  below horizontal). Southern-Nevada / Arizona / Coachella climate
  scope.
- **Mango** (`Mangifera indica`) — South Florida / warm-zone tropical.
  Anthracnose during bloom is the defining disease pressure; prune
  immediately after harvest; low-nitrogen high-potash feeding
  schedule.
- **Guava** (`Psidium guajava`) — South Florida / CA coast. Tolerates
  hard pruning for size control; Caribbean fruit fly controlled by
  bagging at 1-inch fruit size; harvest indicator is color (pink/red
  → peel yellows; white → stays green).
- **Mulberry** (`Morus rubra` native, `M. alba` naturalized / `M.
  nigra` home cultivar) — tolerant of most US climates. Famously
  easygoing: winter pruning to shape, fertilize late winter +
  mid-summer, harvest is "fruit drops when ripe, shake the tree."
- **Pawpaw** (`Asimina triloba`) — native to the Midwest / East,
  hardy USDA 5a–9. Understory species, needs partial shade when
  young. Requires two genetically different trees for cross-pollination
  — insect pollination is notoriously unreliable, hand pollination is
  the reliability upgrade. Harvest is soft-to-the-squeeze in a tight
  3–5 week September window.

## Per-tree template shape

Following the "some trees don't need 5 categories" guidance — we
declare the categories each tree's cited sources actually cover.
Phase E has the thinnest coverage of any phase, so several trees
ship fewer categories deliberately.

| Tree | pruning | protection | feeding | monitoring | harvesting |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Date | Dead-frond pruning (Jan) | — | Spring feeding (Feb–Mar) | Hand pollination + strand thinning + bagging (Mar–Jul) | Stage harvest (Sep–Oct) |
| Mango | Post-harvest pruning (Jul–Aug) | — | Low-N high-K split feeding (Feb + Jun) | Anthracnose bloom fungicide (Jan–Mar) | Color-turn + flesh check (May–Sep) |
| Guava | Size-control pruning (anytime, not Nov–Feb) | — | Every 1–2 months young / 3–4x mature | Caribbean fruit fly bagging (Jun) | Variety-color harvest (summer + early spring) |
| Mulberry | Winter pruning (Jan–Feb) | — | Late-winter + mid-summer (Feb + Jul) | — | Shake-the-tree harvest (May–Jun) |
| Pawpaw | — | — | — | Hand pollination (Apr–May) | Soft-squeeze harvest (Sep) |

Notable omissions:
- **Date** skips protection — cold damage below 20°F is zone-
  boundary, not an annual task
- **Mango** and **Guava** skip protection — same reason, plus the
  overview guide already covers young-tree frost basics
- **Mulberry** skips monitoring — the main pest (white peach scale)
  is controlled by the dormant-oil pass during winter pruning, not
  a separate monitoring task
- **Pawpaw** skips pruning (not covered in cited sources — no
  specific technique for home orchards), feeding (sources say only
  "moderate soil fertility" — no schedule), and protection (native
  tree, handles its native range fine). Ships monitoring (the
  hand-pollination task — critical for fruit set) and harvesting
  only. Two categories is deliberate; inventing a pruning cadence
  not backed by extension would violate the project rule

Per-tree task IDs match Phase A/B/C/D convention:
`<slug>-pruning`, `<slug>-feeding`, `<slug>-monitoring`,
`<slug>-protection`, `<slug>-harvest` (category value `harvesting`).

## Per-tree quirks to capture

- **Date**: three hand-grower jobs that distinguish date from every
  other fruit — (1) hand pollination in March/April, (2) strand
  thinning in May/June to 15–20 fruits per strand + 35 strands per
  stalk, (3) bagging at khalal stage (July) with porous cloth. Prune
  never below horizontal — "pencil pointing" from over-pruning
  predisposes palms to wind break. The harvest is staged ("thinning
  harvest") over 2–3 passes 1–2 weeks apart for Medjool.
- **Mango**: anthracnose at bloom is THE disease call — one or two
  copper/sulfur applications starting when the flower panicle is
  1/4 size and repeating 10–21 days later. Fertilizer shifts
  drastically once bearing — low N, high K (9–15%). Harvest cue is
  the "shoulders and nose broadening" + internal flesh color turning
  from white to yellow. Fruit ripens best off-tree at 70–75°F over
  3–8 days.
- **Guava**: size control is the headline — a guava left unpruned
  hits 12+ ft fast and fruit becomes unreachable. Prune anytime
  except Nov–Feb (cold-sensitive soft growth). Bagging individual
  fruit at 1-inch size is the only effective home control for
  Caribbean fruit fly. Pink/red cultivars harvested when peel turns
  light green to yellow; white cultivars picked full-sized but still
  green for crunch.
- **Mulberry**: the easiest tree in the phase. "Fruit drops when
  ripe and may be harvested by shaking the tree" is literal extension
  guidance. Winter pruning to remove dead/crossing wood is the only
  pruning pass. Fertilize late winter + mid-summer with 1 lb
  10-10-10 per inch of trunk diameter. White peach scale is the one
  real pest and is handled with dormant oil in late winter (same
  window as pruning).
- **Pawpaw**: cross-pollination failure is the #1 reason home
  pawpaws don't fruit. Plant at least two genetically different
  cultivars, and hand-pollinate with a small brush when natural
  insect pollination (flies + early beetles) isn't reliable. Harvest
  window is a tight 3–5 weeks in September — fruit goes from firm to
  soft-when-squeezed over a few days; once picked it lasts 3–5 days
  at room temperature. No registered pesticides for peduncle borer or
  Asian ambrosia beetle — management is "tolerate the loss."

## Sources to use

Per the ranked domain list in `docs/fruit_tree_care_resources.md`:

- **Date**: UNR Extension (Date Palm Gardening Guide for Southern
  Nevada, Pub FS-02-99 / PubID 3217 / PubID 3222), UA Cooperative
  Extension (Arizona Landscape Palms, az1021; az2021 January 2023
  update), UF/IFAS EDIS (ENH-598/ST439 Phoenix canariensis and
  related palm-care pages for general palm pruning rules).
- **Mango**: UF/IFAS EDIS (HS2/MG216 Mango Growing in the Florida
  Home Landscape; ENH563/ST404 Mangifera indica), UF/IFAS Extension
  blog posts (St. Lucie County Managing Anthracnose and Powdery
  Mildew on Mango Tree; St. Lucie County Common Problems of Mango
  in the Florida Home Landscape).
- **Guava**: UF/IFAS EDIS (HS4/MG045 Guava Growing in the Florida
  Home Landscape; ENY-412/IG072 Guava Pests and Beneficial Insects),
  UF/IFAS Gardening Solutions (Guava).
- **Mulberry**: UGA Extension (B992 Minor Fruits and Nuts in
  Georgia), Clemson HGIC (Red vs. White Mulberry in SC). Coverage
  is thin — `researchNotes` will call this out explicitly, matching
  the flag already on the overview migration.
- **Pawpaw**: Ohio State Ohioline (ANR-0187 Pawpaws: An Alternative
  Fruit Crop in the Midwest), Penn State Extension (The Native
  Pawpaw Tree; Pawpaw Fruit in the Garden and the Kitchen), Purdue
  Extension (Pawpaw – the Indiana Banana?). KSU pawpaw website
  (`pawpaw.kysu.edu`) was offline during research, so cited
  secondarily via Ohio State + Penn State references. Coverage for
  pruning/feeding is extension-thin — we ship only the two
  categories backed by cited sources.

Source citation conventions match Phase A/B/C/D:
- `source` column: short semicolon-delimited list (e.g. `'UNR
  Extension; UA Cooperative Extension'`)
- `researchNotes` field: full page titles in doubled-up single quotes

## Per-tree migration shape

One migration per tree — same convention as Phase A/B/C/D.
- Naming: `<timestamp>_guides_<tree>_per_task.sql`
- IDs prefixed by tree slug: `date-pruning`, `pawpaw-monitoring`,
  etc.
- `approved = true` on insert (review happens on SQL diff before
  commit)
- Doubled apostrophes inside the JSON body — grep for `[a-z]'[a-z]`
  before push
