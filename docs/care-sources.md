# Fruit Tree Care Resources

A ranked shortlist of reliable online sources for backyard fruit tree care, balancing institutional authority with strong independent gardening publishers.

## Simple rule for using sources

**Institutional sources** for timing, rates, pests, and regional accuracy.
**High-quality gardening publishers/blogs** for practical explanation, visuals, and homeowner-friendly wording.

---

## Primary backbone: institutional sources

### 1. Local Cooperative Extension office

Best overall starting point because it is most likely to match local climate, pest pressure, chill hours, watering realities, and seasonal timing.

### 2. UC IPM / UC ANR

Especially strong for fruit-tree pests and diseases, plus backyard orchard management and homeowner-friendly diagnosis.

### 3. Oregon State Extension

Excellent all-around fruit-tree resource for home growers, including pruning, thinning, pests, and orchard basics.

### 4. Penn State Extension

Very strong for pruning, training systems, and seasonal timing for home orchard care.

### 5. Washington State University Extension

Particularly useful for organic or lower-input pest and disease management in backyard fruit trees.

### 6. University of Minnesota Extension

Clear homeowner guidance for apples, pears, and stone fruit, including pruning and basic pest awareness.

---

## Secondary layer: high-quality gardening publishers

### 7. Epic Gardening

A strong secondary source for practical explanations, seasonal care checklists, and plain-English how-tos.

### 8. Dave Wilson Nursery

Very useful for backyard fruit tree culture, pruning for size control, and home orchard concepts.

### 9. Homestead and Chill

A solid gardener-educator source for practical, organic-leaning fruit tree care and real-world how-to content.

### 10. GrowVeg

Helpful for simple explanations of techniques like thinning and summer pruning.

---

## Best source by topic

| Topic | Primary source | Secondary source |
|---|---|---|
| Pests and disease | UC IPM, then local/state Extension | — |
| Pruning | Penn State or Oregon State Extension | Dave Wilson Nursery, Epic Gardening |
| Thinning | Oregon State Extension | — |
| Fertilizing and soils | Extension sources (match local soil/climate) | — |
| Watering | Local Extension (soil type and climate matter) | — |
